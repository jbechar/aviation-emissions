Spirit's Liquidation Returns 44 A320neo-family Aircraft to Market, into a Fleet Where a Third Are Already Grounded

Spirit Airlines' liquidation has returned 44 A320neo-family aircraft to two lessors: SMBC Aviation Capital (25) and Jackson Square Aviation (19), directly into a market where almost a third of the global A320neo/GTF fleet is grounded and a single GTF engine pair can out-earn the aircraft it powers.

  • ~635 aircraft (roughly a third of the global A320neo/GTF fleet) remain grounded or in storage awaiting engine maintenance. (Cirium via AirInsight)
  • A GTF engine pair now leases for ~$200K/month each (~$4.8M/year for two), comparable to or exceeding whole-aircraft lease rates for the same airframe. (Cirium via Simple Flying)
  • A GTF replacement engine carries a $15–25M cost; a ~10-year-old A320neo airframe may be worth only $50–75M on the secondary market. Part-out economics increasingly favor engines over intact aircraft.
  • 85 A320neo-family aircraft from Spirit's rejected leases are already in storage at Pinal County, AZ; industry sources expect a portion will be parted out rather than re-leased whole. (Simple Flying)
  • Recovery signal: GTF Advantage received EASA certification in April 2026; full production transition not expected until H1 2028; the shortage is easing but not resolved over the relevant re-placement horizon.
